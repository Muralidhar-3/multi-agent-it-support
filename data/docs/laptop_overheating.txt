Title: Laptop Overheating Issue

Problem:
Laptop becomes very hot during usage, fan runs loudly, and system performance slows down.

Common Causes:
- Dust accumulation in cooling vents or fan
- High CPU usage due to background applications
- Poor ventilation (using laptop on bed or soft surface)
- Outdated drivers or BIOS
- Thermal paste degradation

Solutions:
1. Clean Air Vents:
   Use compressed air to remove dust from vents and fan areas.

2. Check Running Applications:
   Open Task Manager and close unnecessary high-CPU processes.

3. Use Hard Surface:
   Always place the laptop on a flat, hard surface to allow airflow.

4. Update Drivers:
   Update system drivers and BIOS from the official manufacturer website.

5. Cooling Pad:
   Use an external cooling pad to improve airflow.

6. Reduce Load:
   Avoid running heavy applications simultaneously.

7. Thermal Paste Replacement:
   If the laptop is old, consider replacing thermal paste (requires technician).

When to Escalate:
- Laptop shuts down automatically due to heat
- Temperature exceeds safe limits frequently
- Fan not working properly
